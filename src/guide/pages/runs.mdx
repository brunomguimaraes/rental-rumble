# Runs

A **run** is one climb from draft to Champion. You pick a difficulty, draft six
Pokémon, then fight through a seeded ladder of trainers, Gym Leaders, Elite
Four members, and cameo mini-bosses. After every win you may claim **one**
reward — or skip and keep your team as-is. Between some battles, **item
events** offer team-wide relics that stick for the rest of the run.

## Difficulty

Four modes change the ladder length, how forgiving the draft is, how tough the
Champion is, how sharp the foe AI plays, and how many relic shops appear.

| Mode | Battles | Draft skips | Item events | Champion | Foe AI |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Easy** | 7 | 5 | 1 | −10% stats | Sloppy — barely favours strong moves |
| **Normal** | 10 | 3 | 2 | Baseline | Standard |
| **Hard** | 15 | 1 | 3 | +10% stats | Standard |
| **Master** | 17 | 0 | 3 | +25% stats | Perfect — always picks the best move |

**Battles** counts mandatory ladder fights (trainers → special(s) → gyms →
elite(s) → Champion). A rare, optional **bonus challenger** (Prof. Oak) may
appear just before the Champion; you can fight them or skip straight to the
boss — they do not change the ladder length.

Harder wins rank above easier ones on the leaderboard, even if the easier run
finished first. Time only breaks ties within the same difficulty.

## The draft

You build a team of **six** Pokémon. Each step shows **three** fresh cards;
pick one for your roster or **skip** the whole trio to see the next. Skips are
limited by difficulty (see table above). Once you pick a species, no ancestor or
descendant of that line can appear again — branch siblings (different Eevee
evolutions, for example) are fine.

Each card arrives with a rolled **zodiac sign**, **ability**, and sometimes a
**Physical/Energy build** on mixed attackers. Legendary and mythical species
can appear, but only rarely (~18% of drafts include at most one). You also
choose a cosmetic **Poké Ball** for each pick before locking the team in.

## The ladder

Every run's opponents are pinned to its seed. The broad shape:

1. **Roadside trainers** — procedural type-themed squads (count scales with difficulty).
2. **Special cameo** — a famous anime/manga face with a fixed team (Team Rocket, Ash's mom, …). On Easy and Normal there is one, placed before the Gyms. Hard and Master add a **second** special just before the Champion.
3. **Gym Leaders** — type-themed, six-Pokémon teams.
4. **Elite Four** — famous members when available, otherwise procedural.
5. **Champion** — the shared daily boss for your generation bracket.

Opponent tiers carry a small stat edge (Gym ≈ baseline, Elite slightly higher,
special cameos a notch above, Champion highest). Only the Champion also scales
with your chosen difficulty (see table).

## Item events

Relic shops appear **between** battles — never as a ladder step themselves. They
show up after random stages (never before your first fight or immediately before
the Champion), and the exact slots are seed-pinned.

| Difficulty | Events per run |
| --- | --- |
| Easy | 1 |
| Normal | 2 |
| Hard / Master | 3 |

Treasure Hound on your team adds **+1** extra event. Pick one relic from a
small offered set; duplicates and relics you can't use are filtered out. Relics
apply to **every** battle for the rest of the run.

## Post-battle rewards

After **each** win, choose **one** reward or skip entirely. You cannot take two.

### Always available

- **Recruit a Pokémon** — swap one of the foe's team into a slot on yours. The
  recruit keeps its sign and ball. Recruited Pokémon have a small independent
  shiny roll (Fortune on your team nudges the odds up).
- **Evolution Ticket** — evolve one Pokémon already on your team, if it has a
  legal next stage in your generation bracket. Branched lines let you pick the
  evolution.
- **Tweak a Move** — on one team member, drop a move and replace it with any
  other move from that species' legal pool (STAB, coverage, signature, utility —
  see the Pokédex or move guide for the full list).

### Unlocked by special trainers

Beating the run's **last special cameo** opens two extra reward options for
that victory only:

- **Reroll a Sign** — gamble one of your Pokémon's zodiac signs.
- **Reroll an Ability** — change one Pokémon's ability (only species with a
  real ability choice qualify).

Which special counts as "last" depends on difficulty:

| Difficulty | Sign / ability reroll unlocks after… |
| --- | --- |
| Easy / Normal | The single special (before the Gyms) |
| Hard / Master | The **second** special (just before the Champion) |

Both reroll rewards share the same unlock — you still pick **one** reward total.

#### Sign reroll

The new sign is always **different** from the current one. Outcomes are pinned
to the run and stage — leaving the screen cannot re-roll the luck.

- **Ordinary special** (most gag cameos): a weighted roll among the twelve
  common signs, with boosted long-shot odds for a **rare celestial** (~20%) or
  the mythic **Abhijit** (~1%).
- **Strong special** (tougher villain cameos): a **guaranteed rare celestial**
  sign — never a common roll, never Abhijit (the mythic stays a long-shot on
  ordinary rerolls only). Which wanderer you get is still seeded and random.

From the outside every special looks the same; the tier is hidden until the
reveal.

#### Ability reroll

- **Strong special**: **hand-pick** the new ability from the species' pool.
- **Ordinary special**: a blind gamble for a **different** ability from the pool
  (seed-pinned, like the sign reroll).

Species with only one ability cannot use this reward.

## Generation brackets

Runs can lock to a generation (Kanto-only, Johto-only, …) or use the full
National Dex. The bracket filters the draft pool, every procedural foe, and the
daily Champion for that era.
