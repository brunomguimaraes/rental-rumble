# Turn order & the AI

Higher move priority (e.g. Quick Attack) always strikes first; otherwise the
faster Pokémon goes, with ties broken by a coin flip.

Each turn the AI walks a fixed checklist, taking the first option that applies:

1. **Guaranteed KO** — if any move (priority first) is forecast to faint the foe,
   take it.
2. **Taunt a staller** — if the foe packs a heal or setup button, a 50% chance to
   Taunt it, sealing its setup, heals and status moves for a few turns so it has
   to trade damage instead of fortifying.
3. **Heal** — below 35% HP, a 60% chance to use a sustain move like Recover. Each
   heal restores less than the last (the first is biggest), and Recover runs out
   after 3 uses, so two walls can no longer out-heal each other forever. Blocked
   while taunted.
4. **Crack a wall** — when the foe is still above half HP and the best honest hit
   barely dents it, a 70% chance to use Super Fang, lopping off half the foe's
   current HP, ignoring Defense and bulk entirely.
5. **Set up** — above 60% HP and not yet stacked, a 40% chance to buff its stats
   (Swords Dance, Agility, Iron Defense, or a dual-stat Dragon Dance / Bulk Up).
   Blocked while taunted.
6. **Lower the foe's stats** — while the foe is still above half HP, a 35% chance
   to drop one of its stats (Charm saps Attack, Screech melts Defense, Scary Face
   tanks Speed). Blocked while taunted.
7. **Spread status** — against an unafflicted foe, a 35% chance to fish for burn /
   paralysis / poison / sleep / confusion. Blocked while taunted.
8. **Best damage** — otherwise throw the move with the highest forecast damage vs.
   this defender (coverage matters).
